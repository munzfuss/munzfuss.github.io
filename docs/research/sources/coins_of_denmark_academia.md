## Scope-relevant excerpts

### Erich von Pommern (Erik VII of Pomerania) — 1396 (1412) - 1439

Source PDF: pages 79-80.

Eric of Pomerania (ca. 1382 – 3 May 1459), reigning king of
Denmark, Sweden, and Norway 1396-1439 (effectively from 1412 after
the death of Queen Margrethe I).

«During the 1430s the policy of the king fell apart. […] he left
Denmark in 1439 and settled at his castle Visborg in Gotland,
apparently a kind of a "royal strike" which at last led to his
deposition.»

**Coin entries:**

- **Sterling, Lund mint, 0.73 g.** Obverse: Crown in pearled circle.
  Reverse: Footed cross in pearled circle. Reference: Galster 9. VF.
  *Auction: estimate 50 EUR; price realized 180 EUR.*

- **Sterling, Næstved mint, 0.79 g.** Obverse: Crown in pearled
  circle. Reverse: Footed cross in pearled circle. Legend «MONETA
  NESTWED». Reference: Galster 4. VF.
  *Auction: estimate 50 EUR; price realized 160 EUR.*

- **Copper Sterling, Næstved mint, 1.01 g.** Obverse: Crowned E in
  pearled circle. Reverse: Footed cross.
  *(Compilation page truncated mid-entry — full reference cut off.)*

### Royal Council issue 1448 (Rigsrådet)

Source PDF: page 81.

- **Hvid, Malmø mint, 0.73 g.** Obverse: Lion in pearled circle.
  Reverse: Footed long cross over shield. Reference: Galster 22.
  Corrosion, VF.
  *Auction: estimate 100 EUR; price realized 320 EUR.*

Issued by the Danish Council of the Realm (Rigsrådet) during the
interregnum between Christopher III of Bavaria's death (6 January
1448) and Christian I's election (28 September 1448).

### Christian I (1448-1481)

Source PDF: pages 82-83.

Historical-narrative section only. No coin catalogue entries in the
PDF for this reign. Key biographical points relevant to our
`rigsdukatfod` / `nobel_fod` / `rhinsk_gylden_fod` phase-pre-I
context (House of Oldenburg founding):

«Christian I (1426 – 1481), Danish monarch and union king of
Denmark (1448 – 1481), Norway (1450 – 1481) and Sweden (1457 –
1464), under the Kalmar Union. […] Also Duke of Schleswig and
Holstein 1460-81. […] The dynasty he founded, the House of
Oldenburg, remained on the throne of Denmark until 1863, and of
Norway until 1814.»

### John / Hans (1481-1513)

Source PDF: pages 83-84.

Historical-narrative section only. No coin catalogue entries in
the PDF.

«Hans (Danish, Norwegian and Swedish) (2 February 1455 – 20
February 1513), was a Danish monarch and union king of Denmark
(1481 – 1513), Norway (1483 – 1513) Sweden (1497 – 1501), under
the Kalmar Union, and also Duke of Schleswig and Holstein. […] The
three most important political goals of King Hans seem to have
been the recovery of the union, fight against the Hanseatic League
and the making of a strong Danish royal power.»

Note the 1500 Ditmarschen / Battle of Hemmingstedt campaign:

«In 1500 he made a fatal attempt at conquering the Ditmarshes
(Dithmarschen) in Northern Germany, an area which the kings of
Denmark had long viewed as belonging to their realm […]. Together
with his brother Frederick he carried through en large-scale
campaign […], but the Ditmarshers caught most of the army in a
trap after having opened the dykes of the low-land area and
flooded the narrow main road.»

### Christian III (1535-1559) + Frederik II (1559-1588)

Source PDF: pages 89-90.

**Coin entries (both kings, on one auction-lot page):**

- **2 Skilling 1558 (Frederik II 1559-1588).** Obverse: Crowned
  arms of Denmark over long cross. Legend «FRIDERICVS Z:D:G:DANIE».
  Reverse: In circle «.8./SKILLI/NCK DA/:NSKE./1583». Edge
  «NORWEGIE. . SLAVON: GOTO:Q;REX». Reference: H 10 (Fr. II), F/VF.
  *Note: catalogue header date «1558» appears inconsistent with
  reverse date «1583» — likely typo in the compilation; the «1583»
  shown on the reverse is the issue year.*

- **8 Skilling 1583 (Christian III 1535-1559).** Obverse: Crowned
  arms of Denmark over long cross. Legend «CHRISTIANVS D G DANIE».
  Reverse: In circle «+II+/SKILLI/NCK DA/:NSKE./+58+». Edge
  «NORWEGIE VANDA GOTO P Q REX». Reference: H 26. VF.
  *Same compilation ambiguity — the labelling probably swaps the
  two coins' rulers; «H 26» (Christian III Hede catalogue) is
  consistent with the Christian III obverse legend.*

### Frederik II (1559-1588) — Klippe + Speciedaler

Source PDF: page 91.

- **Klippe 1 Mark 1564, Copenhagen mint.** Made during the
  Scandinavian Seven Years' War (3-Crowns War 1563-1570). Obverse:
  Crowned shield with F, in circle. Reverse: «o I o /MARCK/ o 1564
  o». Reference: Hede 14 A; Maillet cf. Tf. XXXI, 38 (there year
  1563). VF.
  *Auction: estimate 200 EUR; price realized 420 EUR.*
  *Context: «During the unsuccessful three-crown war led from
  1563-1570 with Sweden, the mint in Copenhagen had to supply its
  king Friedrich II coins in the total volume of over 3 million
  Marks. […] Muenzmeister Poul Fechtel manufactured 1563-1565
  exclusively gold and silver klippe, since the square pieces
  could be manufactured more efficiently and faster than round
  flans for conventional coins.»*

- **Speciedaler 1572.** Obverse: Armored bust left, divides date,
  in wreath. Legend «FRIDERICVS…» (truncated in compilation).

### Christian IV (1588-1648) — extensive coin catalogue

Source PDF: pages 92-111.

This is the most coin-rich section of the PDF. ~30 individual
auction entries covering:

**Small denominations:**
- **Hvid 1614** (Obv. «Large C with date 1614 inside, in crowned
  pearled circle. NOR VAN GOT REX». Rev. «Swords and scabbards
  forms cross. CHRISTIAN 4 D G D»). Refs H 102B, 102D, 102F. F-VF.
- **Søsling 1602.** Obv. «Crowned C with date 1602 inside, in
  pearled circle. NORVE VANDA GOT REX». Rev. «Sword/scabbard
  cross. CHRISTIANVS IIII D G DAN». Reference: H 86. VF.
- **Søsling** (undated bust type). Obv. «Crowned bust right over
  pearled circle. CHRISTIAN IIII D G DA». Rev. «Crowned Danish
  arms over cross, in pearled circle. NOR VAN GOTO REX». Refs H
  84A, Sieg 15.1. VF-F.
- **Søsling 1624.** Obv. «Crowned bust right». Rev. «In cartouche:
  SOS/LING/1624/+». Reference: H 136. VF-F.
- **Søsling lybsk 1644, Glückstadt mint.** Obv. «Crowned C with 4
  inside, in pearled circle. CHRISTIANVS 4 D G». Rev. «I/SOES/LIN.
  DAN: NV : G: Q: REX 1644». Reference: H 178B. VF-F.
- **Søsling lybsk 1646, Glückstadt mint.** Same type, 1646.
  Reference: H 178B. VF.

**1 Skilling:**
- **Skilling 1595 (Electus-type).** Obv. «Danish arms over cross.
  CHRISTIANVS 4 D G DANIA». Rev. «In circle: *I*/SKILLI/NGL.DA/
  SNKE/*95*. Around edge: NOR VAND GOTO Q REX ELECTVS». Reference:
  H 68. VF-F.
  *Period-historical: «Electus» = elected but not yet crowned —
  Christian IV reached majority and was crowned 17 August 1596,
  so 1595 is his electus-period; ELECTVS legend is diagnostic.*
- **Skilling 1619.** Obv. «Crown. CHRISTIA 4 D G D». Rev.
  «*1*/SKILLI/NG DAI/1619». Reference: H 144B (no, this is
  hulpenning — re-checking compilation).
- **Skilling (hulpenning) 1629.** Uniface; «Crowned C with 4
  inside, in toothed circle. On right is date: 29». Reference:
  H 144B. F.

**2 Skilling / 2 Kroneskilling:**
- **2 Kroneskilling 1621.** Obv. «Crown. CHRISTI 4 D G DAN». Rev.
  «In pearled border: *II*SKILLI/NG DAN/1620» *(sic — issued 1621
  with 1620 reverse die)*. Reference: H 116B. VF.
- **2 Skilling 1618.** Obv. «Crowned Danish arms over cross.
  CHRISTIA 4 D G DAN». Rev. «*II*SKILLI/NG DAN/SKE. NORV VAND GOTO
  REX 1618». Reference: H 79D. EF-VF.

**4 Skilling:**
- **4 Skilling 1608.** Obv. «Crowned half-bust right, in pearled
  circle. CHRISTIANVS IIII D G DA 1608». Rev. «IIII/SKILLI/NK.DAN
  over Danish shield. NORVE VAND GOTO Q REX». Reference: H 97. F.
- **4 Skilling 1617.** Obv. «Crowned Danish arms over cross.
  CHRISTIAN IIII D G DAN». Rev. «In pearled circle: *IIII*SKILLI/
  NG DAN/SKE» (year 1617). Reference: H 100A (Sieg 98 var). VF.
- **4 Skilling 1618.** Obv. «Crowned Danish arms over cross.
  CHRISTIAN 4 D G DAN». Rev. «*IIII*SKILLI/NG DAN/SKE. NORV VAND
  GOTO REX 1618». Reference: H 100A, Sieg 98 var (ex. Hede II,

**4 Skilling Lybsk (Glückstadt mint) — historically notable for

- **4 Skilling Lybsk 1619, Glückstadt mint, 1.80 g.** Obv.
  «Crowned and armored king on horseback right, holding sceptre».
  Rev. «CHRIST IAN • DE[R 4] VAN • GOT[T] GNA • KON[N] IN •
  DEN[NE] MARK[EN]». Reference: Schou p.167, 44; KM 9.1. Good VF.
  *Period note: «Struck 1619, for the Baltic trade — Struck in
  the style of the Russian wire kopeks, for trade with the
  eastern Baltic region.»*

**4 Kroneskilling 1621.** Obv. «Crown over .R.E.F., in pearled
  circle. CHRISTIAN. IIII. D. D. DAN». Rev. «.IIII./SKILLI/NG DA

  H 115C. EF.

**6 Skilling Lybsk:**
- **6 Skilling Lybsk 1625, Glückstadt mint.** Obv. «C with 4

**8 Skilling:**
- **8 Kroneskilling 1620.** Obv. «Crown in circle, with .R.F.P.
  below it. CHRISTIANVS IIII D G DANI». Rev. «.VIII./SKILLI/NG

  GOTO REX 1620». Reference: H 114A. VF-F.
- **8 Skilling 1630.** Obv. «Crowned and armored half-bust right.

**8 Skilling Lybsk (Glückstadt, scandalous «nude female» reverse
type — period-iconographic novelty):**
- **8 Skilling Lybsk 1641, Glückstadt mint.** Obv. «Crowned bust
  of king right over Danish shield. CHRISTIA IIII D G DAN N V G R».

  Reference: H 175A (ex. Zinck 4, 115). VF-F.
