## 1. Christian III's pre-1541 monetary situation

Wilcke 7-4 p. 4 (printed 261). Context before the 1541 Møntordning:

> «Christian III. havde da ingen Møntmester, som han kunde have
> Tillid til. Reynold Junge var bleven afsløret som Bedrager i
> største Stil. Jørgen Koch havde været Oprører for anden Gang og
> kunde derfor ikke komme i Betragtning, selv om han alligevel fra
> Tid til anden blev taget med paa Raad.»

> «I Helsingør boede imidlertid en Borger Povel Fechtel, som den
> 22. Maj 1536 havde faaet Kvittering paa 1538 G. i dansk Mønt, hver
> G. regnet for 2½ M d., hvilke Penge han havde annammet af
> Møntmesteren i Roskilde og igen udgivet paa Kongens Vegne efter
> hans Regnskabs Lydelse. Han har altsaa øjensynlig modtaget
> nymøntede Penge og staaet i Forbindelse med Møntmester Reynold
> Junge, muligvis forestaaet Møntfilialen i Roskilde. Han stammede
> vistnok fra Hamburg og blev nu tilkaldt af Kongen til at lede det
> Rigsmøntsted, som Kongen lod indrette i S. Claræ Kirke og Kloster
> i København, og hvorfra en ny og bedre Mønt skulde udgaa.»

Wilcke's secondary citations at this point (footnotes 1-3, p. 4):

1. «Danske Kancelliregistr., Kbhvn. 1881–82, p. 17.» — Fechtel's
   1536 receipt.
2. «Dsk. Mag. I, 1745, p. 162 ff. E. Pontoppidan: Origines hafn.
   1760, p. 293 ff. C. Bruun: Kbhvn. I, 1887, p. 409 f.» — three
   sources documenting Fechtel's appointment and the Sanct Klare
   Kloster mint conversion.
3. «N.N.U.M. 1947, p. 3.» — biographical medal struck for Fechtel.

## 2. The Møntordning of 20 September 1541 — date, archive, Preamble

Wilcke 7-4 p. 4 (printed 261), the foundational claim — verbatim:

> «**'Dinstagh nach Grucis Anno Domini inn 1541'**, o: 20. (15) Septbr.,
> udgik derefter den Møntordning, som skulde danne Skel mellem
> Middelalder og Nytid i dansk Møntvæsen.»

The Old German chancery-date «*Dinstagh nach Crucis Anno Domini inn
1541*» = **Tuesday after Holy Cross (Day of the Exaltation of the
Holy Cross = 14 September) Anno Domini 1541** = **20 September
1541**. Wilcke's parenthetical «(15) Septbr.» reflects his own
hesitation on which of the two adjacent Tuesdays the ordinance text
intends — but the substantive date is firmly the third week of
September 1541, and Galster's 20. sept. 1541 reading is the standard
modern consensus.

**Archive citation** (Wilcke footnote 4, p. 4):

> «RA. T. K., No. 160. Div. Møntsager. Chr. III.s Historie II,
> Kbhvn. 1778, p. 108 ff.»

Parses as:
- **RA** = Rigsarkivet (Copenhagen)
- **T. K.** = **Tyske Kancelli** — the German Chancery, which
  handled the duchies and German-language royal correspondence.
  Note: the 1541 Møntordning is in the German Chancery archive,
  NOT the Danish Chancery (Danske Kancelli) — likely because the
  ordinance was issued under the post-Reformation administrative
  reorganisation that placed Christian III's monetary regulation
  in the realm-wide Tyske Kancelli rather than the Danish-specific
  series.
- **No. 160** = archive shelf-mark for the «Diverse Møntsager»
  (Miscellaneous Mint Matters) folio.
- **Chr. III.s Historie II, Kbhvn. 1778, p. 108 ff.** — the
  18th-century printed transcription of the ordinance, in Volume II
  of an anonymous *Christian den Tredies Historie* published
  Copenhagen 1778, beginning at page 108. This is the printed
  secondary source for the verbatim text (cited but not
  reproduced in extenso by Wilcke).

**Preamble** (verbatim Danish, modernised by Wilcke to 1950 ortho­
graphy; original Old Danish in chancery hand is at Rigsarkivet T.K.
No. 160):

> «Kongen siger i Indledningen, at han ikke alene har befundet sin
> Mønt i stor Uorden(!), men ogsaa lidt stor Skade paa denne, hvorhos
> fremmede Nationers Oprør har bibragt Mønten stor Forklejnelse.
> Derfor maa Mønten nu have Bestand, saaledes at Menigmand og
> indkommende Købmænd saa meget bedre og '*schiedenntlicher*' kan
> handle med hinanden.»

Wilcke encloses **«schiedenntlicher»** (= «more clearly /
distinguishably», from Old German *scheidentlich*) in quotes to
flag it as a literal Old Danish loan-word from the original
ordinance text — confirming Wilcke had the original in hand and is
quoting a specific lexical choice. The Preamble's substantive
content:

1. **Diagnosis**: the king has found his coinage in great disorder
   («stor Uorden») and has suffered great damage on it; foreign
   nations' rebellion («fremmede Nationers Oprør» — likely
   Christian II's pretender insurrections + the Grevens fejde
   continuation) has further reduced the coinage's standing
   («Forklejnelse»).
2. **Goal**: the coinage must now have permanence («Bestand»), such
   that the common man («Menigmand») and incoming merchants
   («indkommende Købmænd») can do business with one another «so much
   better and more clearly» («saa meget bedre og schiedenntlicher»).

The Preamble therefore frames the ordinance as a **restoration**
(after the 1530s debasements) rather than as an innovation, with an
explicit appeal to commercial functionality across linguistic /
mercantile boundaries.

## 3. Specifications of the 1541 Møntordning

Wilcke 7-4 p. 5 (printed 262), full specification table reconstructed
from Wilcke's printed tabular layout (pypdf flattens it ugly; my
reconstruction matches Wilcke's printed values cell-by-cell):

| Mønt | stk paa 1 Mc brutto | Finhed (Lod) | Tusinddele | stk paa 1 Mc fin (Gyldens) | Bruttovægt | Finvægt | Finvægt i 3 M (= 1 Daler) | Mønterløn pr. Mc fin |
|---|---:|---:|---:|---:|---:|---:|---:|---:|
| **M (Mark)** | **24** | **14½** | **906,25** | **26½** | **9,744 g** | **8,830 g** | **26,490 g** | **1 M d.** |
| ½ M | 48 | — | — | — | 4,872 g | 4,415 g | — | — |
| 4 ß | 96 | — | — | — | 2,436 g | 2,208 g | — | — |
| 1 ß | 106 | 4 | 250,00 | 26½ M c d. | 2,206 g | 0,552 g | 26,496 g | — |
| Hvid | 318 | 4 | — | — | 0,735 g | 0,184 g | — | 2 M d. |
| Penning | 768 | 2 | 125,00 | 32 M c d. | 0,305 g | 0,038 g | 21,888 g | 6 M d. |

Notes on the table:

- **«Stkr. paa 1 Mc brutto»** = number of coins struck from one
  Cölln. Marck of brutto-weight alloy (Marck = 233.856 g).
- **«Finhed»** in Lod (1 Marck = 16 Lod), so 14½ Lod = 14.5/16 =
  90.625 % fineness; converted to Tusinddele (per-mille) = 906,25 ‰.
- **«Stk. paa 1 Mc fin»** = number of coins from one Cölln. Marck of
  fine silver = (stk brutto) / fineness. For the Mark: 24 / 0.90625
  ≈ 26½. This is the **Marken-fin tariff** (mf) of the period.
- **«Finvægt i 3 M»** = fine silver in 3 Marks (which post-1544
  becomes 1 Daler): for the Mark, 8.830 × 3 = 26.490 g. This is the
  per-Daler silver content implicit in the 1541 standard, three
  years before the daler was formally placed at the head of the
  system.
- **«Mønterløn pr. Mc fin»** = mintmaster's seigniorage per Cölln.
  Marck of fine silver minted.
- **The 1 Hvid Mønterløn of 2 M d.** and **the 1 Penning Mønterløn
  of 6 M d.** are roughly 2× and 6× the Mark's 1 M d. — Wilcke notes
  this explicitly: the smaller-denomination seigniorages are
  proportionally higher (the Penning is essentially credit money for
  small-change purposes).

**Internal consistency check.** Marken-fin (mf) per Daler = number
of Daler from one Cölln. Marck of fine silver = 233.856 / 26.490 =
**8.827 stk/Mark fein**. This matches the explicit Hede-page «mf
8.827» empirical attestation on c3h3-3A (1 Mark 1541, brutto 9.744,
finhed 0.906, finvægt 8.830, mf 8.827) cell-for-cell. The
correspondence between Wilcke's transcribed ordinance specification
and the surviving 1541-dated Hede c3h coins is exact.

## 4. Numismatic innovation: coin-name-on-design

Wilcke 7-4 p. 5 (printed 262):

> «Heraf vil ses, at Mønternes Underafdelinger undtagen Penning er
> holdt strængt op til Gyldenens Finvægt. I Mønternes Omskrifter
> fandtes ikke mere det forhadte og mistænkelige: '**MONETA NOVA
> HAFNIENSIS, AALBORGENSIS, MALMOENSIS**' etc., som kendetegner
> Middelalderens ustandselige Møntforringelser med ny daarligere
> Mønt. Paa Mønterne var anbragt deres Værdier **M, 8 ß, 4 ß, 1 ß,
> Penning**, saaledes at der ogsaa paa denne Maade blev brudt med
> gammel Slendrian, der holdt Menigmand i Uvidenhed om, hvad den
> 'nye Mønt' betød. Bl. a. til Møntvæsenets Fremme synes der at
> være udskrevet Landehjælp over Rigerne. Det er som nu først
> indtraadte Renæssancen for Mønternes Vedkommende.»

Substantive points:

- The 1541 ordinance **breaks with medieval coinage convention** of
  inscribing only place-names on coins (MONETA NOVA HAFNIENSIS =
  «new coinage of Copenhagen», etc.) — a convention Wilcke
  characterises as «*forhadte og mistænkelige*» (hated and
  suspicious) because it was associated with serial medieval
  debasements where each new place-name issue was lighter than the
  previous.
- The new design inscribes **the denomination value directly** on
  the coin: «M», «8 ß», «4 ß», «1 ß», «Penning». This is the first
  systematic Danish practice of coin-name-on-design (which Galster
  also documents in his paraphrase: «*For første gang sattes møntens
  navn i præget*»).
- Wilcke frames this as the **arrival of the Renaissance in Danish
  monetary practice** («det er som nu først indtraadte Renæssancen
  for Mønternes Vedkommende»).
- A **Landehjælp** (special land-tax) was levied to fund the
  monetary reform.

## 5. Extension to Norway and the 1544 supplement

Wilcke 7-4 p. 13 (printed 270). After the Norwegian silver-mining
operation at Guldnæs (Telemarken) began producing in 1543-44,
Christian III extended the Møntordning to Norway:

> «Den 24. Februar 1544 meddeler Kongen Alempech, at i hans Rige
> skal betales med Sølv, der skal slaas hele og halve Dalere af den
> Gehalt og Korn, som er befalet i Møntanordningen, som Kongen
> sender med, tillige med Stemplerne, som bruges for Rigsdalere; med
> Markstykker og anden smaa Mønt skulde forholdes efter nærmere
> Ordre.»

> «Denne var dateret 26. September 1544 til Anthoni Alebreken(!).
> Der skulde slaas Rigsdaler og ½ Daler i Halt og Skrot som vor
> Møntordning, der medsendtes, udviser, og oversendtes atter Eysen
> til Daler brug; og skal Markstykker og andre smaa Mønter, hvortil
> ogsaa oversendtes Forbilled, udpræges saaledes, at de kunde bruges
> ved Bjergværket. **Møntordningen, hvorefter unge Jørgen Koch
> skulde arbejde, var dateret 27. September 1544.**»

**Three dates in September 1544**, all part of the same regulatory
package:

| Date | Recipient | Substance |
|---|---|---|
| 24 February 1544 | Anton Alempech (Bergfoged at Norway mint) | Announcement that Norway will mint Rigsdaler and ½ Daler per the enclosed Møntordning + stamps + dies |
| 26 September 1544 | «Anthoni Alebreken» (= Antoni Alempech, the same person, name garbled in Wilcke's transcription with «!») | Detailed instruction: strike Rigsdaler + ½ Daler per the enclosed Møntordning; Markstykker + smaller coins per separate sample-forms also enclosed; product to be used at the bjergværk (mine) |
| 27 September 1544 | Jørgen Koch the Younger (Mintmaster at Skien/Gimsø, then Flensborg) | Møntordning itself, dated one day after the cover letter to Alempech |

Wilcke footnote 2 (p. 13): «**RA. T.K. No. 160. Diverse Møntsager.**»
— the 27 September 1544 Møntordning manuscript is in the same
Tyske Kancelli archive file as the 20 September 1541 Møntordning.

## 6. Specifications of the 27 September 1544 supplement

Wilcke 7-4 p. 13 (printed 270), full specification table — the
**1544 standard is slightly debased** vs the 1541 baseline:

| Mønt | Finhed (Lod) | stk paa 1 Mc brutto | Tusinddele | Bruttovægt | Finvægt | Gyldens Finvægt (= 3 M + 4 ß) | Metalværdi 1875 | Købekraft 1875 |
|---|---:|---:|---:|---:|---:|---:|---:|---:|
| **Gyldengroschen** | **14½** | **8** | **906,25** | **29,232 g** | **26,491 g** | **26,491 g** | 4,19 | 23,28 |
| **M** | **13½** | **24** | **843,75** | **9,744 g** | **8,221 g** | **24,663 g** | 1,30 | 7,22 |
| ½ M | (13½) | 48 | (843,75) | 4,872 g | 4,111 g | — | 0,65 | 3,61 |
| 4 ß | (13½) | 96 | (843,75) | 2,436 g | 2,056 g | — | 0,33 | 1,80 |
| 1 ß | 4 | 118 | 250,00 | 1,982 g | 0,495 g | 23,760 g | — | — |
| Hvid | 3½ | 314 | 218,75 | 0,745 g | 0,163 g | 23,472 g | — | — |

Wilcke's verbatim commentary on the comparison (p. 13):

> «Denne Ordning er noget ringere end den, der udstedtes 1541, og
> blev 1544 gjort fælles for begge Rigerne. Som det vil ses, er
> Detailmønten, om den bruges i Groshandel, delvis Kreditmønt for
> Gylden- eller Dalerværdien.»

Substantive points:

- **The 1544 ordinance is somewhat lighter than the 1541 ordinance**
  («noget ringere»). The Mark Danske finhed drops from 14½ Lod
  (906.25‰) to 13½ Lod (843.75‰) — a ~7 % debasement of the
  Mark.
- **The 1544 standard was made common for both kingdoms** («blev
  1544 gjort fælles for begge Rigerne») — i.e. Denmark + Norway
  now share one Møntordning, whereas 1541 had been Denmark-only.
- **The Gyldengroschen** (= Daler, dated 1544 in Norwegian
  production at Skien/Gimsø) is held at **14½ Lod / 26.491 g
  finvægt** — matching the 1541 Mark-implied 3M Gyldenværdi to
  the gram (26.490 g). So the daler is preserved at the original
  1541 silver weight; only the Mark (and the smaller subdivisions)
  is debased. This is the **structural origin of the post-1544
  Daler-as-anchor convention** Galster paraphrases: 1 Daler = 3
  pre-1541-grade Marks, but the post-1544 Mark is lighter than the
  pre-1541 one, so the «3 Mark = 1 Daler» equivalence holds
  nominally (in Mark counting) but not metrically.
- **Sub-Mark «Detail-mønt» is partially credit money** («delvis
  Kreditmønt for Gylden- eller Dalerværdien») — i.e. the 1 ß and
  Hvid are tariffed at face value above their silver content
  (23.760 g and 23.472 g per 3M-equivalent, vs the canonical
  26.491 g Daler-finvægt).
- The Metalværdi 1875 and Købekraft 1875 columns are Wilcke's own
  modernisation, converting the silver content to 1875 Reichsmark
  metal value and corresponding purchasing-power index. Not
  strictly part of the ordinance.

## 7. Mints under the ordinance and after

Wilcke 7-4 pp. 15–16 (printed 272–273). Christian III's mint network
after 1541:

- **København (S. Claræ Kloster)**: principal royal mint, headed
  by **Povel Fechtel** (from Helsingør, originally Hamburg). Fechtel
  served from 1541 through at least 1556 (per the receipts on
  pp. 31-32 / printed 288-289).
- **Skien / Gimsø Nonnekloster** (1543-1546): Norwegian mint at
  the Gimsø-island former nunnery near Skien, established at
  Bergmester Hans Glaser's recommendation to coin the
  silver-from-Norwegian-mining output. Mintmaster: **unge Jørgen
  Koch** (Jørgen Kock the Younger). The mint closed 1546 when
  Gimsø Kloster burned («Uforsigtighed af Bergmand Hans Widemanns
  Hustru»).
- **Flensborg** (re-opened 1544): Mintmaster **Jørgen Degener**
  (Diegener), with kautionist Tyde Knipensen of Innhausen
  bankrolling 2000 Gylden working capital. Degener died 1546;
  succeeded briefly by Reynold Junge (the same Reynold Junge
  earlier discredited for fraud, here in his final position before
  his own death), then by **Jørgen Kock the Younger** transferred
  from Skien on 22 January 1547 with new Bestalling and
  Møntordning.

## 8. 1547 Flensborg sub-ordinance for Jørgen Kock the Younger

Wilcke 7-4 pp. 25-26 (printed 282-283), verbatim Old German
transcription — Christian III's Bestalling og Møntordning of 22
January 1547 for the Flensborg mint:

> «Nach dem wir unsern lieben getreuen Jurgen Kock vier Jahrlangh
> bestellen und annehmen lassen in den Furstenthumben
> Schleswig-Holstein zu münzen, sonderlich dass er die Münz binnen
> unser Stadt Flensburgk haben und haben soll, Alles laut unser
> den selben gegebene bestallung wollen wir, dass der genannte
> unser Munzmeister sich dieser unser ordnung unnachleslichen halten
> soll, und weill genannte unser Munzmeister guiden und halbe
> guiden groschen, Dobbelte und einfachige ß, syslinge, witten,
> Blaffert und pfennige und kein andere münz in halt. und schrott
> wie folget schlagen und münzen soll, wollen wir, weill an der
> kleine Münze mangell, dass abzeit, wen 1 M Guiden und halbe
> guiden geschlagen, 1 M kleine münze, erst Dobbelte und einfachige
> ß, darnach syslinge und dann blaffert und pfennige soll gemünzt
> und geschlagen werden.»

Substantive ordering: each Marck-worth of Guilders + half-Guilders
must be paralleled by an equal Marck-worth of small-change, in the
sequence: Dobbelte ß → einfachige ß → Søslinge → Blaffert →
Pfennige. The clause is a check against the typical mintmaster
abuse of striking only the profitable larger denominations.

The Bestalling continues, specifying that:

- **Guldgylden** must be struck per the Elector-Princes' Gulden in
  value, halt, schrot and korn (i.e. the standard imperial
  Reichsgulden specification).
- **Sølvgylden + ½ Sølvgylden** as per the Hanseatic-cities
  standard, **OR** — if the Hansestädten do not strike Dalere — at
  14 Lod 1 Quintin fineness (= ~14.06 Lod = 879 ‰), 8 stk per
  blandet lybsk Marck (i.e. heavier than Hansa's later 14²⁄₉-lødig
  / 25.984 g Reichsthaler).
- The mintmaster keeps 2 Grän «for risk» allowance but must not
  use it routinely.
- The 2 ß, 1 ß, ½ ß (Søslinge), Blaffert, and Pfennige must match
  Hamburg-Lübeck-Lüneburg standards.

Wilcke's commentary (p. 26, printed 283):

> «Naar Galster i «Mønt» p. 186 erklærer, at Daleren i Slesvig
> skulde slaas som i Hamburg, Lybeck og Lüneburg, det vil sige,
> 14½ lødig, er dette fra flere Sider urigtigt. Den tyske
> Rigsdaler skulde efter Vedtagelsen 1566 kun være 14²⁄₉ lødig,
> altsaa Bruttovægt 29,232 gr., Finvægt 25,984 gr.»

Wilcke explicitly corrects Galster here: the Flensborg 1547 daler
was struck heavier (14¼ Lod = 26.035 g finvægt) than the post-1566
imperial Reichsthaler (14²⁄₉ Lod = 25.984 g finvægt) — i.e. the
duchies' coinage in the late 1540s was held HIGHER than what would
later become the imperial 1566 / 9-Thaler-Fuß standard. This is the
**direct empirical anchor** for our project's `9_thaler` Müntzfuß
(later anchor 1566 Reichsabschied) — the Christian III pre-1566
duchies coinage already anticipates that standard by ~20 years.

## 9. Wilcke's primary and secondary sources for the 1541 Møntordning

Compiled from Wilcke 7-4 footnotes 1-4 (pp. 4-5) and surrounding
references:

### Primary archive

- **Rigsarkivet, København** — **Tyske Kancelli, No. 160. Diverse
  Møntsager.** The original manuscript of the 20 September 1541
  Møntordning, together with the 27 September 1544 supplement and
  the 22 January 1547 Flensborg sub-ordinance, all in the same
  archive folio. (Wilcke footnote 4 on p. 4 + footnote 2 on p. 13.)

### 18th-century printed transcription

- **«Christian den Tredies Historie», Vol. II** (København 1778),
  pp. 108 ff. — anonymous 18th-c. printed compilation of Christian
  III sources, contains the verbatim 1541 Møntordning text.
  (Wilcke footnote 4 on p. 4.) Likely the same compilation
  referenced as «Chr. III.s Historie I, Kbhvn. 1776, p. 702 ff.»
  (Wilcke footnote 1 on p. 3) for the 1539 Norwegian
  Bjergordnung — a 2-volume Christian-III source-edition by the
  same anonymous editor.

### Other 18th-century printed sources

- **«Danske Magazin», Vol. I** (København 1745), pp. 162 ff. — for
  Fechtel's appointment as Mintmaster and the conversion of S.
