## §1. The Wendischer Münzverein context

The Mark-Lybsk standard that the Husum 1514 ducal coinage sits on
predates Christian I's Schleswig-Holstein duchy by more than a
century. It originates in the **Wendischer Münzverein** — the
monetary alliance of the Wendish Hanseatic cities (Lübeck, Hamburg,
Wismar, Lüneburg, and later Rostock, Stralsund, Greifswald),
formalised through periodic Münzrezesse from the late 14th century
onward.

Canonical reference (cited by Wilcke throughout 7-1 and 7-2): **W. Jesse,
*Der wendische Münzverein*, Lübeck 1928** — particularly pp. 78,
112, 128, 131, 138, 211, 218, 260-261. The Lübisch Mark-Fod metric
documented in Jesse 1928 is what Hertug Frederik adopts at Husum 1514.

### Pre-Husum-1514 Mark-Lybsk evolution (Wilcke 7-2 p. 185, citing Jesse)

The Mark-Lybsk standard drifted across the late-medieval period
before settling into the early-16th-century Hertug-Frederik form:

| Period | Stkr / Cölln. Marck | Lod | ‰ Fineness | Mark Lybsk brutto | Finvægt per Mark |
|---|---:|---:|---:|---:|---:|
| Lübeck 1512-1513 (Hansestäder) | 13 | 14½ Lod | .906 | 18.0 g | 16.22 g fein → 13 Mark / Mark fein |
| Lübeck 1515 | 12 | 14½ Lod | .906 | 19.48 g | 17.64 g fein → 13 ß 4 ß / Mark fein |
| Lübeck 1512 (small change Doppelschilling) | 104 | 15 Lod | .937 | 2.25 g | 2.1 g → 14 ß / Mark fein |
| Lübeck 1522-1530 (4 ß lybsk) | 56-58 | 7½-7¾ Lod | .469-.484 | 4.17-4.03 g | 1.96-1.95 g → 14 ß 9-14 ß / Mark fein |

Wilcke verbatim (7-2 p. 185): «*De lybske Markstykker i Tysklands
Nordprovinser og i Hansestæderne vejede 1512-13 18 gr. og 13 Stkr.
paa den 14½ lødige M med Finvægt 16,22 gr., 13 M paa M fin og 1515:
19,48 gr. og 12 Stkr. paa den 14½ lødige Mark*»

The standard anchor across all variations is **14½ Lod (.906)
fineness for full-Kurant Mark-Lybsk**, with subsidiary tiers
proportionally debased. This .906 figure is the **structural seed**
of the Husum 1514 standard.

## §2. Imperial authorisation chain (1474)

Per Wilcke 7-2 p. 184: **Kaiser Friedrich III's 1474 privilege**
authorised Christian I of Denmark, as Duke of Holstein (under the
1460 Vertrag von Ripen / Treaty of Ribe «*up ewig ungedeelt*»),
to strike gold and silver coinage in the duchy.

Verbatim p. 184: «*I Aaret 1474 meddelte Kejser Friedrich III. Kong
Christian I. som Hertug af Holsten Tilladelse til at slaa Guld- og
Sølvmønt. Hertug Frederik benyttede denne Tilladelse til at slaa
Mønt efter Rigsmøntfod i Byen Husum, som han paa forskellig Maade
følte sig knyttet til.*»

(Translation: «In the year 1474 Emperor Friedrich III granted King
Christian I, as Duke of Holstein, permission to strike gold and
silver coinage. Duke Frederik used this permission to mint coins
according to the Reichsmüntzfod in the town of Husum, to which he
felt connected in various ways.»)

Important nuance — Wilcke immediately qualifies p. 185:
«*Naar det siges, at Hertug Frederik lod Mønten slaa efter
Rigsmøntfod, maa det sandsynligvis nærmere sige: Lybsk Møntfod.*»
(«When it is said that Duke Frederik had the coinage struck
according to the Reichsmüntzfod, it should probably be more precisely
said: Lübisch Mark-Fod.») — i.e. the «Rigsmøntfod» in the 1474
privilege text was de facto the Wendischer-Münzverein Mark-Lybsk
standard, not a separate imperial standard.

## §3. Husum 1514+ Hertug-Frederik ducal coinage

### Mintmaster Jørgen Drewes

Wilcke 7-2 p. 184-185: Frederik I's Husum Møntmester. Career arc
documented:

- **Pre-1523** — chief mintmaster at Husum under Hertug Frederik. Drove
  the 1514 Mark-Lybsk series + the 1522 Husumdaler issues.
- **24 Feb 1525** — writes to Chancellor Claus Gjørdsen requesting
  authorisation for a mint house in Aalborg (RA. D. K. Indk. Breve,
  Vejl. Reg., p. 21, No. 44). Before him, Hans Dringenberg held the
  Aalborg mint.
- **1532** — strikes at the Copenhagen mint «*i Nødens Aaret 1532*»
  («in the year of need 1532», Wilcke 7-2 p. 187 — the year of
  Frederik I's military campaigns).
- **Post-1532** — Mønten moved from Husum to Slesvig (Gottorp area),
  but Drewes remained living in the Husum mintmaster's house
  («Das Herrenhaus», Husum Markedsplads, originally built under
  Christian I).

The Mønthus is documented in Wilcke 7-2 p. 186-187, with photographs
of the Husum market square and the building. Drewes is also the
mintmaster cited on **Bruun-14708 + Bruun-14709** (the two 1514
Husum Mark + ½ Mark lots).

### Husum 1514 silver coinage — full spec table

Source: Wilcke 7-2 p. 187 (PDF page 42 of `wilcke_7-2.txt`):

| Denomination | Year | Stkr / Cölln. Marck | Lod | ‰ Fineness | Brutto g | Finvægt g |
|---|---|---:|---:|---:|---:|---:|
| **1 Mark (1 Mark Lybsk)** | 1514 | 13 | 14½ Lod | .906 | **18.0** | **16.31** |
| **½ Mark** | 1514 | 26 | 14½ Lod | .906 | 9.0 | 8.15 |
| **¼ Mark** | 1514 | 52 | 14½ Lod | .906 | 4.5 | 4.08 |
| Doppelschilling (Db.) | 1514+ | 104 | 15 Lod | .937 | 2.25 | 2.1 |
| 1 ß (Skilling Lybsk) | 1514+ | 106 | 8 Lod | .500 | 2.206 | 1.103 |
| **4 ß lybsk (Lejr ß)** | 1514 | 59 | 6 Lod 1 Qvent | .391 | **3.98** | **1.547** |
| 2 ß lybsk | u.år | (smaller variant; Schou-21) | — | — | — | — |
| ¼ ß lybsk | 1514 | (Schou-8) | — | — | — | — |

The «4 ß lybsk» entry — **the «Lejr ß» (camp shilling / war
shilling)** — is the «4/8 Skilling» referenced in §BY's mention of
the Husum 1514 standard. The naming reflects its issue context
(probably war-finance for Frederik I's pre-1523 ducal campaigns) and
its half-mark structural position (4 ß lybsk = ¼ Mark Lybsk × 16 / 4).

Wilcke verbatim p. 187: «*Marken fra 1514 er vejet til 18,9 gr.,
½ Marken til 9,45 gr. og ¼ Marken til 4,66 gr. samt Dobbeltskillingen
1526 til 3,63 — 3,99 g.*» (The specimens-attested weights are 18.9 g
for the Mark, 9.45 g for ½ Mark, 4.66 g for ¼ Mark, and 3.63-3.99 g
for the 1526 Doppelschilling — all ~5 % above the ordinance brutto,
typical specimen variance.)

### The 1522 Husumdaler — the daler-or-Mark dilemma

Source: Wilcke 7-2 p. 185-186. Two attested variants:

| Variant | Year | Stkr/M | Lod | ‰ | Brutto g | Finvægt g |
|---|---|---:|---:|---:|---:|---:|
| **«Svære Husumdaler»** | 1522 | 6½ | 14½ Lod | .906 | **38.0** | **32.60** |
| **«Almindelig Husumdaler»** | 1522 | — | (same) | (same) | **28.9** | — |

Wilcke verbatim p. 186 — the structural interpretation: «*Den senest
fremkomne »svære Husumdaler« fra 1522 kan herefter næppe være andet
end et dobbelt lybsk Markstykke. Vægten 35,21 gr. passer vel til hele
det ovenangivne System, naar noget Slid og Tidens Tand tages med i
Betragtning. Den almindelige Husumdaler vejet i Møntkabinettet til
28,9 gr., er da et Udtryk for Tvivlen og Kampen om, hvorvidt man
skulde gaa over til Dalerregning eller blive ved den tilvante lybske
Markregning.*»

(Translation: «The latest-appeared "heavy Husumdaler" from 1522 can
hereafter scarcely be anything other than a double Lübisch
Mark-piece. The weight 35.21 g fits well with the whole above system
when wear and the tooth of time are considered. The ordinary
Husumdaler, weighed in the Mønt-Kabinet at 28.9 g, is then an
expression of the doubt and struggle over whether to switch over to
Daler reckoning or remain with the familiar Lübisch Mark
reckoning.»)

**The Husumdaler 1522 is therefore one of the earliest dalers in
Northern Europe** — struck only 5 years after the Joachimstaler 1517.
The «svære» variant is structurally a Doppel-Mark-Lybsk (2 × 18 g
brutto = 36 g, attested at 35.21-38 g per specimen); the
«almindelig» variant at 28.9 g matches the southern-German
Joachimstaler / Reichstaler weight tier. Wilcke reads this as
Frederik I's mint experimenting with the Daler concept while still
notionally anchored to Mark-Lybsk reckoning.

This specimen is in our cache: **Bruun has no Husumdaler 1522 lot**
(the §BJ survey would need to check Galster c2g* / Schou for the
specimens). Numista records limited holdings.

### Post-Husum (post-1523) continuation

Per Wilcke 7-2 p. 186-187, after Frederik I's accession to the Danish
throne (1523), the ducal-zone mint moves from Husum to Slesvig
(Gottorp):

- **1526 Doppelschilling** — minted under Frederik-as-king (king from
  1523) but still on the ducal-zone Lybsk standard: 56-58/M at
  7½-7¾ Lod (.469-.484), 4.17-4.03 g brutto / 1.96-1.95 g fein. Wilcke
  explicitly p. 184: «*1526 Doppelskilling — 3,63-3,99 g — (post-king
  cont.)*».
- **1530 Doppelschilling continuation** — same metric, drift to .484.
- **1532 Klippinge** (per Wilcke 7-2 §2706+, danskmoent.dk page) —
  emergency war-finance issues during Frederik I's pre-Counts'-Feud
  military campaigns; minted partly at Copenhagen (Drewes there in
  «Nødens Aaret 1532») and partly at Gottorp (the «Gottorpklippingen»
  referenced in Wilcke 7-2 p. 188).

The Husum-Gottorp ducal-zone Lybsk standard runs continuously from
1474 (imperial authorisation) through 1523 (Frederik I's transfer)
through ~1533 (his death) — and then through the Counts' Feud / pre-
king Christian III phase until Christian III's 1547 Flensborg
Bestalling formalises a renewed ducal-zone standard
(`christian_iii_flensborg_fod`).

## §4. Frederik I's 1524 royal ordinance — a partial Lybsk import

Source: Wilcke 7-2 p. 192-193 (PDF pages 47-49).

After accession 1523, Frederik I attempted to align the Danish royal
coinage with Hansestädische Lübisch reckoning — but only partially.
The 25 February 1524 royal ordinance set 1 Gylden = 2 Mark Dansk at
12 ß = 24 ß (vs the Lübisch Hansestäder 1½ Mark à 16 ß). Wilcke's
direct quote of the king's Rigsråd statement 1523: «*Mynten ville
udgøre eens og liig ved vores Mynt (o: Hertugdømmets) og udi de
Vendiske Stæder*» («The coinage shall be equal and like our coinage
[i.e. the Duchy's] and in the Wendish cities»).

But on the silver-content side, the royal Sølvgylden / Mark Dansk /
4 Skilling stayed at **14 Lod (.875)** — NOT the Lübisch 14½ Lod
(.906). The Mark-Lybsk-Fod thus remained PURELY a ducal-zone standard
during Frederik I's king phase 1523-1533, even as the Lybsk
*reckoning* (24-ß-per-Gylden) leaked into Danish royal accounts.

This dual-track structure — Lybsk reckoning + Danish fineness on the
royal side, Lybsk reckoning + Lybsk fineness on the ducal side —
becomes the model for Christian III's 1541/1547 dual reform
(`christian_iii_dalerfod` royal + `christian_iii_flensborg_fod`
ducal).

## §5. Forward genealogy — what comes after Husum-fod

The .906 / 14½-Lod ducal-zone standard propagates forward through
five major redefinitions, each preserving the fineness floor + the
Lybsk subdivision:

| Date | Müntzfuß | Ruler / authority | Mint | Fineness | Note |
|---|---|---|---|---:|---|
| **1474** | (imperial privilege to Christian I) | Kaiser Friedrich III | (general) | n/a | Authorises Holstein duchy coinage |
| **1514** | **`frederik_i_husum_fod`** *(proposed)* | Frederik I as Duke | Husum | **.906** | 13 Mark Lybsk / Cölln. Marck rauh |
| **1522** | (same standard, Husumdaler experimental) | Frederik I as Duke | Husum | .906 | Daler-or-Mark dilemma |
| **1526-1532** | (same standard, post-king cont.) | Frederik I as King | Gottorp | .906 (Doppelskilling tier) | Mint moved to Gottorp |
| **1547** | **`christian_iii_flensborg_fod`** | Christian III, Jørgen Kock the Younger | Flensborg | .890⅝ (14¼ Lod) | Slight relaxation; SAME structural lineage (8 Daler/M, Lybsk subdivision) |
| **1571** | (Flensborg-fod tail ends) | Frederik II | Flensborg | — | Standard ends as F2 9-Thaler-Fuß takes over realm-wide |
| **1618** | **`kronemont_chr_iv`** + Glückstadt minting | Christian IV | Glückstadt | .859 | Lybsk-aligned sub-Mark logic migrates to Glückstadt |
| **1813** | **`18_5_thaler`** Rigsbankdaler | Frederik VI | (Rigsbank) | — | 18½ Rigsbankdaler / Mark fein — the Lybsk-Untertheilung lineage culminates |

The structural through-line is the **24-ß-lybsk = 1-Gylden subdivision**
(NOT the 48-ß-danske subdivision used by the Danish royal mint). This
24-ß half-mark structure survives every redefinition and is the
genealogical signature of «Schleswig-Holstein-Helstaten-Münzwesen»
all the way to the 1813 Rigsbankdaler reform.

Per CLAUDE.md project context: «*The Lybsk-aligned sub-Mark logic
survives the end of the standard in 1571 — it migrates to Glückstadt
under Christian IV (Krone-Mønt 1618+) and is formalised under
Frederik III as the Rigsbankdaler standard.*»

## §7. Open research questions

For future deepening when the SH page expansion cycle starts:

1. **Pre-1514 SH ducal-zone (Christian I 1460-1481 + Hans 1481-1513)**:
   What metric did Christian I and Hans strike on, between the 1460
   Vertrag von Ripen and Frederik I's 1474-authorised Husum activation?
   Lange-1 through Lange-10 must document this. **Likely
   pre-Wendischer-Münzverein-aligned (older Lübisch standard or
   Hamburger Mark variants)**. Galster c1g + Hans-era pages on
   danskmoent.dk + Lange 1908-12 paper source needed.

2. **Mintmaster Jørgen Drewes — full career biography**: Wilcke gives
   fragments (Husum 1514+, København 1525 + 1532, Aalborg attempt
   1525). Specific Bestalling text + tenure dates + parallel
   mintmasters? Wilcke 7-2 footnotes cite RA. D. K. Indk. Breve.

3. **The 1522 Husumdaler in numismatic literature**: Wilcke positions
   it as «one of the earliest dalers in Northern Europe» (5 years
   after Joachimstaler 1517). Cross-check Davenport (Dav-) numbers,
   Whaley *European Crowns* if available. Whether Bruun-4279 or other
   1522 specimens exist in the Stack's Bowers parts.

4. **The «Lejr ß» (4 ß lybsk 1514)**: Wilcke calls it a war shilling
   tied to Frederik I's pre-1523 ducal campaigns — which specific
   campaigns? Period sources (Andr. Høyer *Dänem. Geschichte* 1719
   cited by Wilcke) should clarify the issue context.

5. **The Gottorpklippingen 1532+**: Reynold Junge's description per
   Wilcke 7-2 p. 188 — Klipping issues from Gottorp during Christian
   III's pre-king period (he became king 1534 after the Counts' Feud).
   Distinguishing these from the Roskilde/Aarhus Klippinge 1535 (which
   §BY's `christian_iii_grevens_fejde_fod` covers) is important —
   the Gottorpklippingen belong to the ducal-zone lineage, not the
   civil-war cascade.

6. **Pre-1547 Christian III ducal coinage 1534-1547**: Christian III
   became Duke of Schleswig-Holstein 1534 (parallel to becoming King
   of Denmark in 1536 after winning the Counts' Feud). What did he
   strike at Gottorp in the 1534-1547 interregnum, BEFORE the
   Flensborg Bestalling formalised the 14¼-Lod Sølvgylden standard?
   The Bruun-14783 «Ducat ND 1534» (Galster-130 / .764) is one
   data-point — but more silver issues likely exist (pending §AZ).
