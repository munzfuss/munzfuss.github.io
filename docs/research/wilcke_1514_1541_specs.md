# Wilcke 1950 ordinance specifications — 1514-1541 Danish-Norwegian coinage

> **§AZ Tier 1 companion** to `data/seed/bruun/denmark_pre_1541.yml`.
> While the Bruun-derived seed provides PER-SPECIMEN brutto-weight +
> NGC-grade + cross-refs, this dossier captures the ORDINANCE-LEVEL
> specifications that Wilcke 1950 documents — fineness (Lod or Karat),
> stkr-per-Cølnsk-Mark, Bruttovægt + Finvægt theoretical, with verbatim
> quotes from the legal acts. These ordinance specs feed into
> `data/shared/fuesse.yml` Müntzfuß-definitions when §BF wires up the
> Christian-II-Lovkompleks / Christian-III-Dalerfod Müntzfüße.
>
> All page references are to Wilcke 1950 (J. Wilcke, *Renæssancens
> Mønt- og Pengeforhold 1481-1588*, Copenhagen 1950), local cache at
> `scripts/cache/wilcke/renaessancens_moent_1950/pages/wilcke_7-*.txt`.

Source: Wilcke 7-2 p. 152-153, citing Suhm + Rigsarkivet T.K. Indk. Breve.

| Denomination | Stkr / cølnsk Mark | Lod / Karat | ‰ Finhed | Bruttovægt g | Finvægt g |
|---|---:|---|---:|---:|---:|
| **Nobel** | 16 | 23½ Karat | 0.979 | 14.616 | 14.310 (≈) |
| **Rhinsk Gylden** | 72 | 18 Karat | 0.750 | 3.249 | 2.437 |
| **Sølvgylden** | 8½ | 14 Lod | 0.875 | ~27.5 | ~24 |
| Skilling | 96 | 5¾ Lod | 0.359 | 2.436 | 0.875 |
| Hvid | 222 | 4½ Lod | 0.281 | 1.053 | 0.296 |

Companion acts in the 1514-1515 Lovkompleks (per Wilcke p. 153 + p. 481):
- **3 August 1514** — Møntordning for Norge (extends Danish ordinance to Norway under Kalmar Union)
- **Paasketid (Easter) 1515** — Kvittering (compliance receipt from Rigsråd)
- **24 August 1515** — Sjælland åbent Brev (Sjælland-specific renewal with adjusted Skilling fractions)

### Christian II's drift 1515-1518

Wilcke p. 153 notes that Skillinge 1515 (without halo) are «betydelig tungere» (significantly heavier) than the ordinance specified — actual specimens deviate from Brev Dines Blicher metric upward in 1515-1516, then back down.

### 1518 Klipping ordinance

Source: Wilcke 7-2 p. 157+. Christian II's tarif-Klipping series at radically debased fineness — Wilcke describes as «*ydel Kobber*» (essentially copper).

## §2. Frederik I 1524 Royal Ordinance

### 25 February 1524 ordinance (København)

Source: Wilcke 7-2 p. 184-187. Verbatim p. 184: «*Vi Fr. … at slaa, i Guld: Nobler (den vegne Mark skal holde 23½ Karat og skraade 16 Stkr.), Rinske Gylden (18 Karat, 72 Stkr.) …*»

|---|---:|---:|---:|---:|---:|
| **(Db.) Nobel** | 16 | 23 Karat | 0.9583 | 14.616 | 14.007 |
| **Sølvgylden** | 8 | 14 Lod | 0.875 | 29.232 | 25.578 |
| ½ Sølvgylden | 16 | 14 Lod | 0.875 | 14.616 | 12.789 |
| ¼ Sølvgylden | 32 | 14 Lod | 0.875 | 7.308 | 6.394 |
| **Rhinsk Gylden** | 72 | 18 Karat | 0.750 | 3.249 | 2.437 |
| 2 Mark Danske | 11 | 14 Lod | 0.875 | 21.259 | 18.602 |
| 1 Mark (12 ß) | 22 | 14 Lod | 0.875 | 10.629 | 9.301 |
| ½ Mark (6 ß) | 44 | 14 Lod | 0.875 | 5.314 | 4.650 |
| 4 ß | 78 | 14 Lod | 0.875 | 3.0 | 2.625 |
| 4-Hvid ß | 96 | 5 Lod 2 grän | 0.319 | 2.438 | 0.778 |
| Hvid | 300 | 4 Lod | 0.250 | 0.779 | 0.195 |

A separate 26 February 1524 act for «14 %» small-change subtype: 3 Lod 6 grän, ~0.20 g fineness — see Wilcke p. 187.

### Frederik I 1527 Goldgulden Malmö (Galster 59)

Source: Wilcke p. 216 (passing reference to specimens). NOT a separate ordinance — minted under the 1524 royal ordinance framework. Brutto 3.249 g, 18 Karat / 0.750, Finvægt 2.437 g — matches the 1524 Rhinsk Gylden spec exactly.

### Frederik I 1531 Ungersk Gylden / Goldgulden (Galster 46)

Source: danskmoent.dk/fr/f1g46.htm. **Specimen confirms 23⅛ Karat = .986 fineness, 3.49 g brutto, 3.44 g fein** — this is HIGHER fineness than the 1524 ordinance's Nobel spec (23 Karat = .9583). The 1531 Ungersk Gylden is therefore a Reichsdukatenfuß-class issue (67/Cölln. Mark fein), not within the 1524 ordinance framework. Likely a separate authorising Brev (not yet sourced in Wilcke 1950).

### Frederik I 1532 Sølvgylden series (Galster 47-52)

Source: Wilcke p. 184-187 + danskmoent.dk f1galst.htm. The 1532 issues mark the END of Frederik I's reign and represent a final consolidation:

| Galster # | Denomination | Year | Brutto g | Material |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 47 | 1 Sølvgylden | 1532 | 37.751 | Silver |
| 48 (×2) | ½ Sølvgylden (Pillars / Statues obv) | 1532 | 14.98 | Silver |
| 48 | 1½ Sølvgylden | 1532 | 45.58 | Silver |
| 52 | ¼ Sølvgylden | 1532 | 7 | Silver |
| 45 | 1 Nobel | 1532 | 14.375 (.979) | Gold |

The Nobel 1532 fineness (.979) is between the 1524 23-Karat (.958) and the 1531 23⅛-Karat (.986) figures — suggests intermediate refinement attempt.

## §3. Frederik I as Duke of Schleswig (Husum + Gottorp)

### Husum 1514+ (Frederik I's pre-king duchy phase)

Source: Wilcke 7-2 p. 186 (separate spec table for ducal-zone coinage). Frederik I struck duchy coinage as Duke of Schleswig from 1490s until 1523 accession to Danish throne.

| Year | Denomination | Stkr/M | Lod | ‰ | Brutto g | Finvægt g | Mintmark |
|---|---|---:|---:|---:|---:|---:|---|
| 1514 | Mark (1 Mark lybsk) | 13 | 14½ Lod | 0.90625 | 18.0 | 16.31 | Husum |
| 1514 | ½ Mark | 26 | 14½ Lod | 0.90625 | 9.0 | 8.15 | Husum |
| 1514 | ¼ Mark | 52 | 14½ Lod | 0.90625 | 4.5 | 4.08 | Husum |
| 1514 | 4 ß lybsk | 59 | 6 Lod 1 Qvent | 0.3906 | 3.98 | 1.547 | Husum |
| 1522 | Husumdaler («sværeste») | 6½ | 14½ Lod | 0.90625 | 38.0 | 32.60 | Husum |
| 1522 | Husumdaler («almindelig») | — | (same) | (same) | 28.9 | — | Husum |
| 1526 | Doppelschilling | — | — | — | 3.63-3.99 | — | (post-king cont.) |

Mintmaster: **Jørgen Drewes** (cited on Bruun-14708 + 14709 1514 Mark + ½ Mark lots).

The **14½-Lod (.90625) fineness** at Husum 1514 is **exactly the Christian-III 1541 København baseline** (per `moentordning_1541.md` §4.1). The Husum 1514 spec PREDATES Christian III's 1541 Møntordning by 27 years and establishes the same fineness floor — suggesting Schleswig-Holstein ducal coinage was at the «Schleswig-tradition» standard that Christian III later adopted realm-wide in 1541.

### Christian III Gottorp 1534 Ducat (Galster 130)

Source: Wilcke 7-3 + Bruun-14783 (lot 14259). Christian III's pre-king Holstein duchy phase: **«1 Ducat» struck at 0.764 fineness, 3.19 g brutto**. NOT Reichsdukatenfuß metric — this is a Rhinsk-Gylden-metric coin NAMED «Ducat», sourced from the Wettinian / Saxon duchy gold-coin tradition rather than the Hungarian Reichsdukat lineage.

Legend «MO:NO:AVREA:SLESVICENSIS» («New gold money of Schleswig»). Pre-dates the 1564 Bremerholm «I DOK KATE» Reichsdukatenfuß-metric Dukat by 30 years; metric and lineage are DIFFERENT (per §BJ source survey + `denmark_pre_1541_source_survey.md`).

### 1537 Joachimstaler at Copenhagen

Christian III's first attempt at a heavy daler-format issue (4 years before the 1541 Møntordning establishes the dalerfod). **Three weight tiers** documented in Bruun (lots 1005, 13060, 11165 = 28.69-28.94 g; lot 13061 = 14.11 g half-daler) — all at the 14½-Lod / .90625 fineness Wilcke spec for Joachimsdaler 1537.

Mintmaster: **Reynold Junge** (career: Aarhus 1535 Klippinge → Roskilde 1535-37 → Copenhagen 1537+ until exposed as fraudster, replaced by Povel Fechtel 1541 per `moentordning_1541.md` §7.2 mintmaster's-oath context).

### 1535 Klippinge series at Roskilde + Aarhus

Bruun lots 13057-13059 (Aarhus 2 Mark Klippe Galster-92B, Roskilde 2 Mark Galster-97, Stockholm 4 Skilling Sieg-24) — all at .406-.437 fineness (heavily debased), Klippe (square-blank) format struck for war finance.

## §5. Christian III 20 September 1541 Møntordning

Full dossier at `docs/research/moentordning_1541.md`. Summary spec (Wilcke 7-4 p. 116, file lines 116-185):

| Denomination | Stkr/M | Lod | ‰ | Brutto g | Finvægt g |
|---|---:|---:|---:|---:|---:|
| 1 Mark Dansk | 24 | 14½ Lod | 0.90625 | 9.744 | 8.830 |
| 8 Skilling (½ Mark) | 48 | 14½ Lod | 0.90625 | 4.872 | 4.415 |
| 4 Skilling (¼ Mark) | 96 | 14½ Lod | 0.90625 | 2.436 | 2.208 |
| 1 Skilling | 192 (computed) | (varied) | (varied) | ~2.0 g | — |
| Hvid u.år | 318 (computed) | 4 Lod | 0.250 | 0.735 | 0.184 |
| Penning | (varied) | 2 Lod | 0.125 | 0.305 | 0.038 |

**Marken-fin (mf) per Daler = 8.827** (Hede c3h3-3A explicit) — 1 Daler = 233.856 / 8.827 = **26.494 g fein** per Cølnsk Mark.

Mintmaster: **Povel Fechtel** (binds via Müntzmesteres Eydt + Wardins Eydt — see `moentordning_1541.md` §7.2). Mint: Klarekloster, København (royal centralised mint replacing the war-period decentralised network).

## §6. 1544 supplement (debasement under Christian III)

Source: Wilcke 7-4 + `moentordning_1541.md` §4.5. Forordning 27 September 1544 debases the dalerfod metric:

| Denomination | Year | Lod | ‰ | Brutto g | Finvægt g | Marken-fin |
|---|---|---:|---:|---:|---:|---:|
| 1 Mark Dansk (Hede 3B) | 1544-1555 | 13 Lod | 0.843 | 9.744 | 8.221 | mf 9.481 |

Per-Daler fein drops from 26.494 g (1541 baseline) to **24.665 g** (1544 debased) — ~7 % fineness reduction. Bruun-4282 (1541 Hede-3A specimen, brutto 9.92 g) sits at the 1541 baseline; Hede 3B variants 1544-1555 at the debased level.

## §8. Müntzfuß-definitions §BF roadmap

The spec tables above feed into `data/shared/fuesse.yml` definitions:

1. **`christian_ii_lovkompleks_fod`** (proposed name, pending §BD verdict): mf calibrated from Sølvgylden 8½ stkr/M = 27.5 g brutto + 14 Lod fineness → ~24 g fein per Sølvgylden. Phases:
   - Phase A1 (1514-1515 Lovkompleks baseline)
   - Phase A2 (1518 Klipping debasement)
   - Phase A3 (1523 final consolidation)

2. **`frederik_i_dalerfod`** (proposed name): mf 8.0 from 25 Feb 1524 Sølvgylden spec (8 stkr per cølnsk Mark at 14 Lod = 29.232 g brutto / 25.578 g fein per Sølvgylden). Phases:
   - Phase A1 (1524-1531 royal Copenhagen ordinance)
   - Phase A2 (1527-1531 Malmö Goldgulden — separate Rhinsk Gylden track)

3. **`frederik_i_husum_fod`** (Schleswig-Holstein duchy): mf calibrated from Mark Lybsk 13 stkr per cølnsk Mark at 14½ Lod = 18.0 g brutto / 16.31 g fein. Phases:
   - Phase A1 (1514-1522 royal Husum, mintmaster Jørgen Drewes)
   - Phase A2 (1522 Husumdaler tariff)
   - Phase A3 (post-1523 continuation into Frederik I's king phase)

4. **`christian_iii_grevens_fejde_fod`** (proposed name): the Civil-war debasement table is NOT a single Fuß but a CASCADE of debased variants. Could be modelled as:
   - Single Fuß with multiple weight-tier phases (preferred — cleaner)
   - OR multiple sub-Müntzfüße for each fineness-variant (overcomplicated)
   1537 Joachimstaler at 14½ Lod fits OUTSIDE this — it's a tariff-coin attempt closer to dalerfod precursor.

5. **`christian_iii_dalerfod`** (main 1541 Müntzfuß, per §BF + `moentordning_1541.md` §8): mf 8.827 baseline + 9.481 debased.

Each of these definitions can be machine-built from the spec tables above once §BF starts wiring up `fuesse.yml`. The Bruun-seed entries in `data/seed/bruun/denmark_pre_1541.yml` are then promoted into curated entries pointing at the right `fuss:` and `phase:` slot.
